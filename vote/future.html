Katie Goclan Truett Anstey Candidate 3
Katie Goclan

Who are you? (answer as you wish...)
I am Katie Goclan. I have been interested in robotics and engineering since middle school when I finally got to join an engineering class. I am the kind of person who is always willing to help a friend out whether they need help with homework or just need someone to talk to. I have not been involved in many clubs in the past because I was scared to join but now that I have found a club that I am really passionate about I want to help it out by running for a leadership position.

Why do you want this position?
I want to be president because I want to help improve the club and keep things running smoothly. I have already helped Mr. Hulsey out with things for the club like setting up competitions and talking with other teachers about their clubs and how they would set things up. Becoming president would allow me to continue contributing to the club and work for the club more then I have been.

Why do you think you are qualified for this position?
I think I am qualified for this position because I have been doing a similar job to being the president of the club without being the president. I have communicated with Mr. Hulsey a lot with ideas for the club, I have collected forms and money from other students and turned it into Mr. Hulsey, and I have helped Mr. Hulsey in running the organization smoothly.

What will you uniquely bring to this position?
I am the kind of person who can always find something to do voting me president would allow me to find random small jobs for other students to do if they do not know what to do. I have not been a leader in another club but I have been a leader in the robotics club.

How involved have you been with robotics in the past?
With the robotics club, I have been the unofficial vice president since we did not have elections and no one else ran for the position of vice president. Since I am the unofficial vice president I have had meetings with Mr. Hulsey and the other club leaders during cav-time and I have met up with Mr. Hulsey during my free periods and lunches to work on things for the club.

Do you have any conflicting commitments for next year? If so, how will you manage them?
Other organizations that I am a part of are track, cross country, and NHS these groups have options to meet before school which I plan on doing so I can attend more after school robotics meetings. Track and cross country do meet sometimes on weekends, but I would talk with my coaches ahead of time to let them know I have something going on for robotics that is much more important.

What are your goals for the robotics program?
I want to improve communication between the different teams. This year we had three teams and A team and B team were great at communicating with each other however since the C team was a freshman team it was hard for A team and B team to communicate with them outside of class. I would like to be president so I can continue working for the club and creating small other projects such as a lock-in, team dinners, and summer meetings and practices for 2020-2021.

How will you achieve these goals as president?
I would achieve these goals by working with the communications director to set up communication between the team captains of all the teams and making sure everyone in each team can communicate with each other. We already were making plans for a lock-in on the night of announcing the new game and I would use those plans and communicate with teachers of other programs that have done a lock-in to figure out what we would need to do this. Team dinners would be a great way for the teams to bond and learn more about each other so they could become a more efficient team, they wouldn't have to be dinners they could also be going to High 5 and playing games. Summer meetings and practices would help teams prepare for competitions in the fall and winter like other schools with great robotics programs. I would work on communicating with both the coaches and assistant principals on unlocking the school to work on robots over the summer.

What previous leadership experience do you have?
I have been the unofficial vice president of the robotics club and have attended all leader meetings for the robotics club. Besides that, I was part of a group of five students selected to present a project called SchoolsNext at several competitions. SchoolsNext was an architecture program where groups of middle school students would design a school for the future. My group ended up getting third place worldwide partly because of all the hard work the five leaders put in during the school year and summer.

Describe your leadership style.
My leadership style could be described as democratic. I like to know the facts of situations and what others think of something before I make a decision. However, I will always choose what is best for the team and come up with ideas that will benefit the whole club and not just one team. I am able to get things done in any way as long as I see that it will benefit the club as a whole.

How do you handle conflict?
If there was a conflict between two teams over parts I would first try and find more of the part that the teams need. If I am not able to find more parts I would have the two teams tell me why they need the part. I believe all conflicts can be solved through communication if everyone is willing to work things out.
